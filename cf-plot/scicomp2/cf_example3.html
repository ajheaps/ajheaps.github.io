Example 3 - Data slicing and meaningÂ¶

1. Select a field and show the axis values.

>>> u=f.select('eastward_wind')
>>> u
<CF Field: eastward_wind(time(1), pressure(23), latitude(160), longitude(320)) m s**-1>

>>> u.item('pressure').array
or
>>> u.item('Z').array
array([ 1000., 925., 850., 775., 700., 600., 500., 400.,
300., 250., 200., 150., 100., 70., 50., 30.,
20., 10., 7., 5., 3., 2., 1.], dtype=float32)

2. Slice the data at 500mb.

>>> u500=u.subspace(Z=500)

>>> print u500
eastward_wind field summary
---------------------------
Data           : eastward_wind(time(1), pressure(1), latitude(160), longitude(320)) m s**-1
Axes           : time(1) = [1964-01-21 00:00:00]
               : pressure(1) = [500.0] mbar
               : latitude(160) = [89.1415176392, ..., -89.1415176392] degrees_north
               : longitude(320) = [0.0, ..., 358.875] degrees_east

3. Make a zonal mean.

>>> zonalmean_u=u.collapse('mean', 'longitude')

Here we see that the cell methods states that the longitude axis has had a zonal mean operator applied.

>>> print zonalmean_u
eastward_wind field summary
---------------------------
Data           : eastward_wind(time(1), pressure(23), latitude(160), longitude(1)) m s**-1
Cell methods   : longitude: mean
Axes           : longitude(1) = [179.4375] degrees_east
               : time(1) = [1964-01-21 00:00:00]
               : pressure(23) = [1000.0, ..., 1.0] mbar
               : latitude(160) = [89.1415176392, ..., -89.1415176392] degrees_north

Various operators are available within the collapse method in cf-Python

method Description
'max' or 'maximum' Maximum
'min' or 'minimum' Minimum
'sum' Sum
'mid_range' Mid-range
'range' Range
'mean' or 'average' or 'avg' Mean
'sd' or 'standard_deviation' Standard deviation
'var' or 'variance' Variance
'sample_size' Sample size
'sum_of_weights' Sum of weights
'sum_of_weights2' Sum of squares of weights
